About Me

I was born in Camden NSW and grew up on the mean streets of Woodridge QLD, and I’ve been working in Brisbane as a Software Engineer for over 6 years. When I’m not at work, I have various other interests such as hanging out with my daughter, brewing beer and mead, playing my guitar, growing herbs and vegetables in my and messing about with my commodore 64 (ok, it’s an emulator but I’m old enough that I grew up with one).

As a software engineer, I’ve had various roles including developing enhancements for government and private clients, providing 24 hour support for mission critical applications, and boring stuff like re-enabling users whom I’ve previously been asked to disable.

My passion is in open source, and as such, most of my personal and professional development is with Java and Scala. I also occasionally contribute to Stack Overflow and plan to contribute to other open source projects.

About this site

I chose the name ‘Keep Surprising Yourself’ because that’s the message that I try to tell myself everyday. I wanted to come up with a name that would be somewhat inspiring to others but something personal that meant something to me and not some tacky generic inspiring message. I think the thought we all get from time to time is how to become the person that we want to be, and for me it was all about the little achievements I’d made when I actually tried something I’d initially thought was too hard and the random acts of kindness I’d performed occasionally. Not outlandish stuff, but just little things that had made a difference in mine or the life of someone else. I’d surprised myself at the feeling of achievement or fulfilment at these little things, so I chose this name to remind me to keep surprising myself.
